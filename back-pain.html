Back Pain Relief
Neck, upper/mid-back, and lower-back stretch routines.
Home Back Pain Relief Workout Builder

Quick guidance

These are general mobility suggestions. If you have numbness, weakness, radiating pain, recent trauma, fever, unexplained weight loss, or symptoms persist/worsen, consult a clinician.

Neck
Cervical mobility + trap/levator relief
Upper / Mid-Back
Thoracic mobility + scapular mechanics
Lower Back
Hip mobility + gentle lumbar decompression

Neck (5–8 minutes)

Goal: reduce tension in upper traps/levator scapulae and restore comfortable range of motion.

Chin Tucks
30–60s
  • Stand tall; gently “double-chin” straight back (no head tilt).
  • Hold 2–3 seconds; repeat 8–12 reps.
Upper Trap Stretch
30–45s/side
  • Sit tall; one hand holds chair/seat for gentle downward traction.
  • Opposite hand guides head away (ear toward shoulder) until mild stretch.
Levator Scapulae Stretch
30–45s/side
  • Turn head ~45° away from tight side, then look down toward armpit.
  • Use hand to gently increase stretch; keep shoulder relaxed.

Upper / Mid-Back (6–10 minutes)

Goal: thoracic extension/rotation + better shoulder blade movement.

Cat–Cow
60–90s
  • On hands/knees: alternate rounding and arching the spine slowly.
  • Move through comfortable range; breathe steadily.
Thoracic Extension (Chair or Foam Roller)
60–90s
  • Support upper back on chair back or foam roller; gently extend over it.
  • Avoid forcing the lower back; think “open chest.”
Open Books (Thoracic Rotation)
8–10/side
  • Side-lying with knees bent; rotate top arm across body to open chest.
  • Keep knees together; rotate through upper back.

Lower Back (6–10 minutes)

Goal: offload lumbar area by improving hips/hamstrings and using gentle flexion/extension.

Knee-to-Chest
30–45s/side
  • Lie on back; bring one knee toward chest; keep other leg relaxed.
  • Only mild stretch—avoid pain.
Child’s Pose
45–60s
  • Kneel, sit back toward heels, reach arms forward.
  • Breathe slowly into the low back.
Hip Flexor Stretch
30–45s/side
  • Half-kneeling lunge; tuck pelvis slightly; shift forward gently.
  • Keep ribs down; stretch should be in front of hip.
